A Guide to Secure SaaS Acquisitions: The Four Essential Questions CIOs Should Pose When Assessing Computer Vision AI Providers for Environmental Health and Safety

As CIOs have driven digital transformation efforts within their organizations in recent years, computer vision AI technologies tailored for environmental health and safety (EHS) applications have rapidly transitioned from being relatively unknown to essential tools for workplace safety. The increasing adoption of these solutions marks significant progress in bridging the long-standing EHS innovation gap. However, this surge in popularity also presents CIOs with the challenge of navigating a broader array of potential vendors, often accompanied by more complex and confusing marketing claims than ever before.


Selecting the right vendor requires thorough evaluation of not just technological capabilities but also a range of factors, including ethics, data management, and operational effectiveness. Here are four critical questions that CIOs should pose to potential vendors to assist in their decision-making process:


1. Who has access to our images and videos?

Why it matters: Transparency and security are critical when handling sensitive data. It's important to examine whether the vendor depends on third parties for crucial tasks, such as data labeling. Any involved third parties must comply with strict security measures to mitigate the risk of data breaches. However, confirming these practices can be difficult, and the exposure to third-party risks may continue to be a concern.


Moreover, I have witnessed the challenges that can occur when third-party involvement hinders the prompt processing of hazard detection and alerting functions. It's crucial to verify that your vendor maintains strong, direct control over data processing or, at the very least, has solid agreements with all parties involved to protect data integrity and guarantee operational responsiveness.

2. How do you guarantee a varied dataset and uphold ethical practices in AI?

Why it matters: The effectiveness and fairness of AI-driven solutions are largely contingent on diverse datasets and ethical development standards. These datasets should include a broad range of environmental conditions, equipment types, physical processes, and human factors, such as varying skin tones, body sizes, and clothing styles. This diversity enables AI systems to perform effectively across different real-world situations and helps to mitigate biases that may result from using homogeneous data.

The solution's capability to adapt and integrate multiple AI models without sacrificing performance is crucial for ensuring continuous visibility into the complete range of hazards that your workplace may face.

Why it matters: Initial testing and ongoing validation of accuracy are essential for the successful implementation and operation of any computer vision AI-driven solution. This cooperative approach aids the system in evolving to meet the actual safety requirements of your workplace.


Above all, it's crucial to recognize that selecting the right computer vision AI vendor for your organization and EHS function necessitates a thorough evaluation of not just their technology but also their ethical standards, integrity, and dedication to operational excellence. This choice carries significant implications that cannot be emphasized enough. However, with a detailed and informed approach, CIOs can ensure they find and collaborate with vendors who are not merely service providers but genuine partners in the pursuit of a safer, healthier, and more successful workplace.
